Placid Seas of Sameness
Psychology
Emotions
Thu, 03 Nov, 1994 00:00

Word for the day:

--Aphorism:
  A concise statement about a principle.

An aphorism about matrix multiplication might go like this: Matrix multiplication, unlike the multiplication of real numbers, is not commutative. Or, an aphorism concerning soap might be: Don’t rub soap in your puppie’s eyes lest it go blind.

A BIG RODENT… [arabic phrase]

No, actually – THE big rodent. Just a touch of arabic to brighten the night. A thought I was speculating upon last night went somehow similar to this:

(by the way – A big rodent would be [arabic phrase].)

Thoughts, as you grow, ideas and concepts, virtues, vices and desires waft at first simply, then with a greater and greater entropy.. These “emotions” reach a point of nigh-uncontrollability, possibly early in the adolescent years. There are so many teens who are rebellious – they don’t think parents, school, whatever is fair. They are going “crazy”. CRAZY! It is just the jumble of emotions, careening and whirling through the young mind, causing a lashing out at order around. Blame is easily placed, but the sources are very hard to find. Fickleness is prominent. Emotions vacillating like a hanky in the wind unteach any logical thought. Finally, this “phase” (as many might dub it), passes to an extent and certain emotional ruts are tread, with only minor, occasional upheaval.

A popular notion is that all of this chaos, and then, eventually, “moderated” EMOTIVE ENERGY cannot be actually controlled by its effectee, but is, instead, subject to some phantom power call “moods”.

I’m not in the mood to do this…

I’m not in the mood to do that…

blah blah blather…

It is my humble opinion that these chaos of emotions, these placid seas of sameness, can be grappled and molded into productive energy. Bah on the bastards who think they are controlled by moods! Moods are, in fact, just emotions that shape the whole emotional state of a person (meta-emotions?), and feelings, as we all know, are just chemical responses in the brain triggered from similar circumstances to past experiences erupting the same. Build! Build! Build! And finally, as the mind has so many references to certain “feelings”, it can pick from the dominant ones more and more as life progresses, giving a few cycling “moods”. Chemical brain spooge can be countered very efficiently with other chemical brain spooge. Just hammer the malleable emotions that shape you into a driving force. Use them! (see ‘psychoderelict’)… I believe this is possible with more ease than most choose to admit. After all, it’s easier to just ride the rapids then fight them, but, eventually, a calm stream of non-moving water is where you end up.
