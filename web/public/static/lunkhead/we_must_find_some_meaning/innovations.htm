Music in progress

These are songs I have worked on over the last months. A few are Robert Calvert covers, which, when finished and accompanied by their companion Calvert covers, will be compiled as a sort of "tribute". Tony and I have been dreaming about/working on this for quite some time.
Please send comments and critiques to me.

Sweet Entropy (excerpt)

This is quite experimental and incomplete (as well as rather defunct at this point). It was originally going to incorporate all of the lyrics which are Sweet Entropy, but so far has only included a few lines. Unfortunately (or maybe fortunately since i am the one doing the singing), the lyrical part is not included here as it is rather rough.

She Ain't Rappaport

Tony sent me the lyrics to this and i wrote music. No actual version with vocals is extant, however, at this point. You can, though, listen to this meandering instrumental thing which is a sketch of the verse and chorus. The fuzzy guitar part is the actual melody line suited for the verse, so, if you like, you can whip out the lyrics and sing along. Just for the general loopiness of it, I have also here a proposed bassline for the entertaining instrumental of the song.

Brave New World

This is simply a dual guitar part encompassing the first verse and chorus. I suppose that would make it a power ballad. Tony is going to eventually do the vox.

The Rah Rah Man

This Calvert tune is mostly complete. It needs the thud of skullwarper, I believe. If you listen carefully, you may hear the mating call of a yak somewhere in the song's midst. Those silly yaks.

All the Machines are Quiet

Okay, so I added quite a bit to this song. You would, too, if you had insomnia for a week. That last instrumental is based on a whole tone scale in A. Quite a bit of this song is incomplete.
